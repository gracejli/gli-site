met for the fourth book club with sarah, addy, lucia: "perfection" by vincenzo latronico
never has a book felt more relevant to our moving lives and our decisions that guide them - sarah in spain, addy moving around, lucia and I in los angeles

talking about using the third person instead of the first person 
as a way that both distances yourself from the characters, losing empathy
but also seeing how the world impacts your choices, and how the ever changing world 
will always affect your life and your direction 

so in a way it's kind to yourself to remind yourself of the third person 
and write about yourself in the third person 

and, lastly, to stray away from consumption of a city and the importance of ties 
work that ties you to people, being in a place with anchors (family, friends)
